================================================================================
THREE INDEPENDENT LAYERS
================================================================================

LAYER 1 — PRODUCT (Travel marketplace + service fulfillment system)
  What it is:     Application for matching travelers with destination guides.
  What it does:   Discovery, messaging, itinerary coordination, trip fulfillment.
  What it is NOT: A payment system. A token platform. A fund custodian.
  TTG role:       None. Product operates without TTG.

LAYER 2 — PROTOCOL (USDC escrow + dispute + reputation engine)
  What it is:     Planned on-chain settlement and record infrastructure.
  What it does:   Lock, release, refund trip funds in USDC; dispute handling;
                  reputation and identity records tied to protocol events.
  What it is NOT: A token layer. A TTG settlement path. A mixed-asset custody system.
  Asset:          USDC only for user trip funds.
  TTG role:       None.

LAYER 3 — TOKEN (TTG — governance only)
  What it is:     Planned configuration vote token (not deployed).
  What it does:   Vote on non-fund protocol settings: escrow policy version,
                  dispute rule parameters.
  What it is NOT: A payment token. An escrow asset. A product access key.
                  A claim on escrow balances or trip payments. An offered or solicited token.
  TTG role:       Configuration votes only. No financial function. No incentives.

Rule: Three separate systems. No shared fund path. Symbol: TTG only.

================================================================================
FUND SEPARATION
================================================================================

User trip funds (only user fund layer):
  Traveler --USDC--> Escrow contract --USDC--> Guide

TTG (token layer only):
  TTG --vote weight--> Non-fund protocol configuration proposals only

Independence (explicit):
  • USDC escrow is the only user trip fund layer.
  • TTG is not used in any payment, deposit, lock, release, refund, or dispute step.
  • TTG has no claim on escrow balances or trip payments.
  • Product and protocol operate without TTG.
  • No arrow connects TTG to user USDC in escrow.
